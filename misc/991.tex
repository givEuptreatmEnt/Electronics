\section{Variables}
\subsection{Store variable}
\bt{shift} \arrow \bt{RCL} (\bt{STO}) \arrow \bt{A}, \bt{B} ... (Do {\bf not} press \bt{ALPHA}) \\
\xe \bt{3} \bt{$\times$} \bt{5} \bt{=} \bt{shift} \bt{STO} \bt{A} \arrow Store $15$ in $A$.
\subsection{Use variable}
\bt{alpha} \arrow \bt{A}, \bt{B} ...

\section{Solver}
In comp mode, use \bt{alpha} \arrow \bt{CALC} to enter \texttt{=}, \bt{alpha} \arrow \bt{)} (\bt{X}) to type $x$, and you can enter an equation with respect to $x$. Then press \bt{shift} \arrow \bt{CALC} to compute. You have to give an initial value $x_0$. If you wish to change your last equation (Or probably you mistype \bt{=} and an error show up), use $\leftarrow$ to navigate back.

\section{Complex}
\subsection{Enter complex mode}
\bt{MODE} \arrow \bt{2}
\subsection{Imaginary number}
\bt{ENG} 
\xe \bt1 \bt+ \bt3 \bt{i} \arrow Input $1 + 3i$, the multiplication sign could be omitted
\subsection{Operator}
\bt{shift} \arrow \bt2 includes $\arg$, $\bar{z}$, $(x, y) \leftrightarrow (r, \theta)$. For $\abs{z}$, simply use \bt{shift} \arrow \bt{hyp} ( \bt{abs} ).

\section{Equation solver}
\subsection{Enter equation mode}
\bt{MODE} \arrow \bt{5}, select what type you want.
\xe to solve
\[
  \begin{bmatrix}
    3 & 1 & 4 \\
    1 & 5 & 9 \\
    2 & 6 & 5
  \end{bmatrix}%
  \begin{bmatrix}
    x \\ y \\ z
  \end{bmatrix} = 
  \begin{bmatrix}
    3 \\ 5 \\ 8
  \end{bmatrix}
\]
Press \bt{MODE} \bt5 \bt2, then fill in

\[
  a = \begin{bmatrix}
    3 \\
    1 \\
    2 
  \end{bmatrix},
  b = \begin{bmatrix}
    1 \\ 5 \\ 6 
  \end{bmatrix},
  c = \begin{bmatrix}
    4 \\ 9 \\ 5
  \end{bmatrix},
  d = \begin{bmatrix}
    3 \\ 5 \\ 8
  \end{bmatrix}
\]

\section{Matrix}
\subsection{Enter matrix mode}
\bt{MODE} \arrow \bt{6}, select what type you want.
\subsection{Enter value}
\bt{shift} \bt4 \bt1 to change dimentions and values, \bt{shift} \bt4 \bt2 to change only value. \\
\subsection{Call value}
\bt{shift} \bt4 \bt3, \bt4, \bt5 to use $A, B, C$.\\
\bt{shift} \bt4 \bt6 is the latest answer.

\subsection{Operator}
\bt{shift} \bt4 \bt7 \arrow $\det A$ \\
\bt{shift} \bt4 \bt8 \arrow $A^{T}$ \\
\bt{$x^{-1}$}  \arrow $A^{-1}$ \\
